Questions to grapple with in preparing for Exam 2

  • Under what conditions might we expect a citizen to exert “voice” against a state that has in the past instituted some policy against her interests? Under what conditions might we expect the citizen to exit? To remain loyal to the state? Why?
  • What is the collective action dilemma? What does it say about the likely calculation of an individual citizen about whether to participate in voting, protest, donating to a political cause, etc?
  • Why do people bother to turn out to vote? What kinds of factors might lead people to vote, and generally to participate in politics?
  • Why might socio-economic status often be positively correlated with political participation? 
  • Are there conditions under which socio-economic status might not be correlated with political participation, or might even be negatively correlated with political participation? Why under those conditions?
  • In the films, An African Election and The People’s Choice, what did you observe about the reasons people were making different choices about whom to vote for and whether to vote?
  • Do you expect ethnic diversity to have consequences for democratic politics? Under what conditions? What kinds of consequences?
  • What kinds of evidence might suggest that partisanship is operating as a social identity in a particular country/time period? 
  • Why do some countries exhibit a greater number of political parties than others do?
  • How would you assess whether a country has high levels of party system institutionalization or not?
  • Why might some countries have a higher level of party system institutionalization than others?
  • Why is electoral fraud difficult to measure? What are some of the ways social scientists have tried to measure it?
  • In the films, An African Election and The People’s Choice, what did you observe about how elections were administered? How did political actors try to prevent electoral malfeasance and ensure electoral integrity (or conversely, to engage in it)? What were some of the challenges to administering elections that the films raised?
